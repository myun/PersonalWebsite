About

While I was an undergraduate at Stanford, I happened to take courses in web design, Java and C++ although my original chosen major was Biology. I absolutely fell in love with programming and became fascinated by code, but it was too late for me to change majors by then. Although I graduated with my original major in Biology, I wanted to return to programming.

I then discovered Hackbright Academy, a 12-week program designed to help women become awesome programmers. Although I was skeptical at first, after seeing how successful the program was at instructing previous classes and how well-established the alumnae were in their new jobs as software engineers, I took a chance. During the first half of Hackbright, I picked up Python, gained experience with pair programming, and learned to work with relational databases, Flask, and JavaScript. In the second half of Hackbright, I used everything I learned (and more!) to build my final project, Edibles, which was a recipe recommendation web app. If anything, Hackbright taught me how to quickly and efficiently teach myself new programming skills/techniques, something I heavily relied on while working on the final project.

I absolutely love programming and aspire to continue developing as a software engineer. Now that I have graduated from Hackbright, I am looking forward to beginning this new stage of my career!
